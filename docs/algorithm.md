# Algorithm Notes

This document describes the equations implemented by the Rust + WebAssembly
calculator. For a function-level map from equations to Rust code, see
`docs/rust_formula_mapping.md`.

## Eddy-Current Model

For the coaxial symmetric-coil case, the electric field generated by each coil
is evaluated with a Hankel-form integral. The implementation discretizes the
`lambda` integral once and evaluates the full field map with matrix products.

$$
u(\lambda)=
\sqrt{\lambda^2-\omega\mu_0(\omega\varepsilon-j\sigma)}
$$

with

$$
\varepsilon = \varepsilon_r\varepsilon_0,\qquad
\omega = 2\pi f
$$

The discretized field kernel is:

$$
E(\rho,z)\approx
\sum_k w_k J_1(\lambda_k\rho)J_1(\lambda_k r_\mathrm{mean})
\frac{\lambda_k}{u(\lambda_k)}
\exp[-u(\lambda_k)d_z]
$$

where `d_z` is the axial distance from the evaluation point to the source coil.

The winding is represented by:

$$
r_\mathrm{mean}=r_\mathrm{out}-\frac{d(N-1)}{2}
$$

and by the turn-radius sum:

$$
S_r = Nr_\mathrm{out}-\frac{dN(N-1)}{2}
$$

The seawater domain is split into the middle region and one side region:

$$
z_\mathrm{middle}\in[0,h],\qquad
z_\mathrm{side}\in[-h_\mathrm{side},0]
$$

The middle region height is the coil gap `h`; it is not an independent input.

For one region, the self and cross integrals are:

$$
I_\mathrm{self} =
\int\!\!\int
\left(|E_p|^2+|E_s|^2\right)\rho\,d\rho\,dz
$$

$$
I_\mathrm{cross} =
\int\!\!\int
2|E_p||E_s|\rho\,d\rho\,dz
$$

The coefficient scale factor is:

$$
F = \frac{2\pi\omega^2\mu_0^2\sigma}{4}
$$

The factor `sigma / 4` comes from:

$$
P = \int_V \sigma |E|^2\,dV
$$

and the `1/2` coefficient in the electric-field expression.

The four eddy-current coefficients are:

$$
A = F I_{\mathrm{self},m},\qquad
B = F I_{\mathrm{cross},m}
$$

$$
C = F I_{\mathrm{self},s},\qquad
D = F I_{\mathrm{cross},s}
$$

The total eddy-current coefficient used in the loss model is:

$$
K_\mathrm{eddy}(\theta)
= A+B\cos\theta+2C+2D\cos\theta
$$

The factor of two accounts for the two symmetric side regions.

## Mutual Inductance Estimate

When automatic `M` is enabled, the calculator estimates mutual inductance with
the Bessel integral form:

$$
M(r,a)=\mu_0\pi r a
\int_0^\infty J_1(kr)J_1(ka)e^{-kz}\,dk
$$

For a symmetric planar spiral, the radial winding is approximated as a uniform
annulus:

$$
r_\mathrm{in}=r_\mathrm{out}-d(N-1)
$$

The double radius integral is evaluated after swapping the integration order:

$$
M \approx
\frac{\mu_0\pi N^2}{(r_\mathrm{out}-r_\mathrm{in})^2}
\int_0^\infty e^{-kz}
\left[
\int_{r_\mathrm{in}}^{r_\mathrm{out}} rJ_1(kr)\,dr
\right]^2 dk
$$

This is a design estimate. Measured or FEM-extracted mutual inductance should
be used when available.

## Automatic Lambda Grid Recommendation

The Hankel integrand contains oscillatory Bessel terms such as
$J_1(\lambda\rho)J_1(\lambda r_\mathrm{mean})$. When the radial integration
domain grows, these terms oscillate faster as functions of $\lambda$. A fixed
coarse `lambda` grid can then alias the oscillations and overestimate the
integrated eddy-current loss.

When `autoLambdaGrid` is enabled, the calculator first recommends the upper
limit from the smallest radial coil feature:

$$
\lambda_{\max,\mathrm{rec}} =
\min\left(
3000,\,
\max\left[
1000,\,
\frac{10}{d},\,
\frac{8}{\delta}
\right]
\right)
$$

where the seawater skin depth is:

$$
\delta=\sqrt{\frac{2}{\omega\mu_0\sigma}}
$$

The recommendation is rounded up to the next multiple of `10`.

The sample count is then chosen from a Nyquist-style condition on the fastest
expected Bessel-product oscillation:

$$
\Delta\lambda \lesssim
\frac{\pi}{1.1(r_\mathrm{sea}+r_\mathrm{mean})}
$$

so:

$$
n_{\lambda,\mathrm{rec}} =
\min\left(
6000,\,
\max\left[
640,\,
\left\lceil
\frac{1.1\,\lambda_{\max,\mathrm{rec}}
(r_\mathrm{sea}+r_\mathrm{mean})}{\pi}
\right\rceil+1
\right]
\right)
$$

This recommendation mainly protects large integration domains, for example
multi-meter `rSea` values. The user can still disable `autoLambdaGrid` and
enter manual values for convergence studies.

## Loss Model

The calculator assumes symmetric primary and secondary sides, double-sided LCC
compensation, and a fixed target transferred power. Absolute efficiency is
meaningful only when `M`, `Lf`, resistances, and target power come from the
same physical design.

For a given angle:

$$
P_\mathrm{trans}=
\frac{MU^2\sin\theta}{\omega L_f^2}
$$

so:

$$
U^2=
\frac{P_\mathrm{trans}\omega L_f^2}{M\sin\theta}
$$

The loss terms are:

$$
P_\mathrm{coil/filter} =
\frac{2U^2(L_f^2R+M^2R_f)}{\omega^2L_f^4}
$$

$$
P_\mathrm{cap} =
\frac{2U^2\left[(M^2+L_f^2+2ML_f\cos\theta)R_{cf}
+R_cL_f^2\right]}{\omega^2L_f^4}
$$

$$
P_\mathrm{eddy} =
\frac{U^2K_\mathrm{eddy}(\theta)}{L_f^2\omega^2}
$$

$$
P_\mathrm{mosfet} =
\frac{4M^2U^2R_\mathrm{dson}}{\omega^2L_f^4}
$$

The total loss and efficiency are:

$$
P_\mathrm{loss} =
P_\mathrm{coil/filter}+P_\mathrm{cap}+P_\mathrm{eddy}+P_\mathrm{mosfet}
$$

$$
\eta =
\frac{P_\mathrm{trans}}{P_\mathrm{trans}+P_\mathrm{loss}}
$$

The UI reports both each loss term's share of total loss and its share of total
input power.

## Optimal Angle

The optimum is solved from the stationarity condition of the normalized total
loss:

$$
\frac{d}{d\theta}L(\theta)=0,\qquad
\frac{\pi}{2}<\theta<\pi
$$

The solver scans the interval for a sign-changing bracket, applies bisection
when a bracket is found, and otherwise minimizes the absolute stationarity
residual over the same interval.

## Scope

The current open-source demo implements the coaxial symmetric-coil case.
Misalignment and offset field mapping should be added as a separate model mode.
